# Zero-Shot vs Grounded Use

## Zero-shot knowledge
Zero-shot knowledge is what the AI produces from general training when you do not provide the needed course-specific materials, examples, constraints, or time frame.

## Grounded use
Grounded use means the task is shaped by the actual course materials, local context, constraints, and output expectations that define the instructional situation.

## Use zero-shot sparingly
- brainstorming early ideas
- low-stakes rough drafting
- generic structural help where course specificity is not yet required

## Do not rely on zero-shot for
- grading or feedback
- policy-sensitive syllabus work
- course-specific summaries
- assignment redesign tied to real learning objectives
- accessibility work where missing details matter

## Signs that you need grounding
- the task depends on a syllabus, rubric, reading, or assignment prompt
- the answer could change based on student level or term context
- institutional policy or local course values matter
- you would need to review the output carefully for hallucinated specifics
